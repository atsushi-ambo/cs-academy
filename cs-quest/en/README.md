# 🎮 CS Quest 〜An Adventure Tale of Computer Science〜

**🌐 言語 / Language: [日本語](../README.md) ・ English (this page)**

> Welcome, adventurer.
> This is the "Continent of Computation (Computer Science)."
> From here you will journey through 8 worlds, conquer dungeons, and defeat bosses,
> all while mastering **real, university-level computer science**.
>
> The way you play is an RPG. The substance is hardcore CS. Anyone can begin, but those who clear it become the real deal.

This course reorganizes the learning content of [Coding Interview University](https://github.com/jwasham/coding-interview-university)
as "a real university CS course × an RPG."
Each world has a **Lecture**, **Quests (exercises)**, and a **Boss Battle (skills exam)**.

---

## 📜 Rules of the Game

### 1. Create Your Character

Copy the [character sheet](character-sheet.md) and start your own adventure log.
Each time you clear a quest, check it off and add up your XP (experience points).

### 2. XP and Levels

| Action | XP Earned |
|---|---|
| 📖 Finish reading a lecture | 10 XP |
| ⚔️ Clear one quest (exercise) | 10–30 XP (stated in the quest) |
| 💻 Clear an implementation quest (write code and make it run) | 50 XP |
| 👹 Win a First Continent boss battle | 100 XP |
| 👹 Win a Second Continent (advanced undergraduate) boss battle | 150 XP |
| 👹 Win a Heavens (graduate level) boss battle | 200 XP |
| 🏰 Conquer a hidden dungeon | 150 XP |

| Level | Cumulative XP Required | Title |
|---|---|---|
| Lv 1 | 0 | Apprentice Adventurer |

| Lv 30 | 2,400 | Navigator of Graphs |
| Lv 40 | 3,200 | Sage of Machine Code |
| Lv 50 | 4,000 | 🏆 Computer Science Hero (First Continent cleared) |
| Lv 60 | 5,500 | Swordsman of Proofs |
| Lv 70 | 7,000 | Architect of Systems |
| Lv 80 | 8,500 | Sage of Theory (Advanced Undergraduate cleared) |
| Lv 90 | 10,500 | Researcher of the Heavens |
| Lv 99 | 13,000 | 🎓 Grand Sage of Computer Science (all domains conquered) |

### 3. Boss Battle Rules

- Take on boss battles **with nothing in front of you** (close your lecture notes)
- You don't need a perfect score — **70% or more** wins
- If you lose, return to the lecture and train again. **Retry as many times as you like** (real learning is born from repetition)

### 4. Choose Your Equipment (Programming Language)

Implementation quests require a weapon for writing code. Recommended starting weapons:

- 🗡️ **Python** — a light, easy-to-handle dagger. Ideal for beginners
- ⚔️ **Java / C++** — heavy but powerful greatswords. A staple even in big-company interviews
- 🏹 **JavaScript** — a bow you can use anywhere. If you have a browser, you can fight

For details, see the original [language resources](https://github.com/jwasham/coding-interview-university/blob/main/programming-language-resources.md).

## 🎓 This Is a "Real Course"

It wears the skin of a game, but the content it covers maps to actual university CS curricula.
**From the First Continent (undergraduate fundamentals) to the Second Continent (the full advanced undergraduate requirements), to the Heavens (graduate specialization)** ——
it's designed to map one-to-one with the actual lectures of Harvard (CS50), MIT, UC Berkeley, Stanford, and CMU,
so through self-study you can reach **undergraduate-to-graduate level**.

- Each lecture proceeds in the order **definition → intuition → analysis → implementation**, the same structure as a university lecture
- The quest exercises are at the level actually asked in real exams and technical interviews
- Links to videos and textbooks connect to the curated resources of the original [Coding Interview University](https://github.com/jwasham/coding-interview-university)
  (public lectures from MIT, Stanford, UC Berkeley, and more)

### Mindset (Words of the Tutorial Elder)

> You don't need to think "I'm just not smart enough…"
> Successful engineers are smart, but **many of them carry the anxiety that they aren't smart**.
> Don't be misled by tales of geniuses. Only those who keep at it steadily reach the Demon Castle.
> —— For details, see the original's [Don't feel you aren't smart enough](https://github.com/jwasham/coding-interview-university#dont-feel-you-arent-smart-enough)

### Daily Play Style (Daily Quests)

1. Read the day's themed lecture (watch the video if there is one)
2. Solve the quests (exercises)
3. For implementation quests, write the code — **writing it on a whiteboard or paper before typing it in boosts your interview skill**
4. Record problems you got wrong in your "retry list" and challenge them again 3 days later (spaced repetition)

### The Big Picture of the Three Continents (from self-study to undergraduate-to-graduate level)

- 🏝️ **First Continent (Worlds 1–8 + the Demon Castle)**: coding interviews and undergraduate fundamentals. Complete this first and you'll be a full-fledged engineer
- 🌄 **Second Continent (Worlds 9–14)**: discrete math, OS, DB, compilers, theory of computation, advanced algorithms. Covers the requirements of a university CS department, completing a foundation equivalent to a "bachelor's degree"
- ☁️ **The Heavens (GRAD 1–4 + Graduation Ceremony)**: distributed systems, security, machine learning, architecture. The frontier of graduate study, research, and industry

Each continent can be tackled independently if you wish, but the foreshadowing (underlying themes) pays off more the more you progress in order.
Conquering it all takes months to years —— but that means it carries the same weight as a real degree.
