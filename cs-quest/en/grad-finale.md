## 🧵 The "Underlying Themes" Threading the Continents

The true value of real university education is that the subjects **come to look connected.**
Recall the themes you met again and again on your journey:

- **"Write the log first" (WAL)**: OS journaling (World 10) → DB transactions (World 11) → distributed consensus Raft (GRAD 1). The same idea appeared three times at different scales
- **Abstraction and layering**: network layers (World 8), compiler front/back ends (World 12), the architecture abstraction stack (GRAD 4). And **abstraction leaks** (Spectre, GC pauses, caches)
- **The diagonal argument**: cardinality of sets (World 9) → the halting problem (World 13). "Feed something its own input" and a contradiction is born
- **Caches and locality**: hardware (World 7) → DB buffers (World 11) → GPU memory bandwidth (GRAD 4)
- **Recursion = induction**: algorithms (World 6), proofs (World 9), parsers (World 12), backpropagation (GRAD 3)
- **"Giving up correctly"**: NP-completeness (World 13) → approximation algorithms (World 14) → ML heuristics (GRAD 3)
- **Graphs are everywhere**: shortest paths (World 5), register-allocation coloring (World 12), max flow (World 14), neural networks (GRAD 3)

If you can see these, you have become not "someone who memorized subjects" but "**someone who sees the world through computer science.**"
