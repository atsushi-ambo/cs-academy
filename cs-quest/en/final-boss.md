## ⚔️ The Second Gate: The Whiteboard Hall (coding exam)

There are no IDEs in the Demon Castle. Write code that runs, **on paper or a whiteboard**, with no compiler.
30 minutes per problem. When done, run 3 test cases through it by hand and desk-debug.

1. Binary search on a sorted array (loop version and recursive version)
2. Reverse a linked list
3. Merge two sorted arrays
4. Find the first non-repeating character in a string
5. Maximum depth of a binary tree
6. Determine whether a path exists between two vertices in a graph
7. Number of ways to climb a staircase (DP)
8. Determine whether any pair in an array sums to k

**Victory condition**: complete 6 of 8 with no syntax errors and no boundary bugs (off-by-one, empty input, null).

> 💡 Want more training? Head to the original repo's [Coding question practice](https://github.com/jwasham/coding-interview-university#coding-question-practice)
> and practice sites like LeetCode. One easy-to-medium problem per day is excellent training.

## ⚔️ The Third Gate: The Hall of Discourse (design and explanation exam)

Write no code. **Explain out loud for 5 minutes each.** Recording yourself and listening back doubles the effect.

1. Explain "what happens from typing a URL to the page rendering," touching as many layers as you can
2. Design "find the top 10 most frequent URLs in billions of access log entries," justifying every data-structure choice
3. Explain hash tables — mechanism and collision resolution — to "someone who knows nothing about them"
4. For your own quicksort implementation, explain the worst case and its mitigation, stability, and memory use

## 👹 The Deepest Chamber: The Demon Lord "Technical Interview" (mock interview)

**Summon one real human** (a friend, colleague, or online mock-interview service) and run a 45-minute mock interview.

Rules of the Demon Lord battle (= the etiquette of real interviews):

1. **Confirm the problem out loud** (ask about requirements, input ranges, edge cases)
2. **State a naive solution verbally first**, with its complexity
3. **Discuss improvements before** writing code
4. **Narrate your thinking** while writing (silence gives the Demon Lord an opening)
5. When finished, **run tests yourself, unprompted**
6. Summarize time and space complexity at the end

These six forms are detailed in the original repo's [Interview Process & General Interview Prep](https://github.com/jwasham/coding-interview-university#interview-process--general-interview-prep).

**Victory condition**: the Demon Lord crumbles when your interviewer says, "I'd want to work with you."
